◆ scale_qp()

template<typename Pbm >
auto smooth::feedback::detail::scale_qp ( const Pbm &  pbm)

Re-scale a QuadraticProgram.

Parameters
pbmproblem \( (P, q, A, l, u) \) to rescale.
Returns
tuple (spbm, s, c) where spbm is a scaled problem \( (\bar P, \bar q, \bar A, \bar l, \bar u)\).

The scaled problem is defined as

  • \( \bar P = c S_x P S_x \),
  • \( \bar q = c q S_x \),
  • \( \bar A = S_e A S_x \),
  • \( \bar l = S_e l \),
  • \( \bar u = S_e u \),

where \( S_x = diag(s_{0:n}), S_e = diag(s_{n:n+m}) \).

The relation between scaled variables and original variables are

  • Primal: \( \bar x = S_x^{-1} x \),
  • Dual: \( \bar y = c E_x^{-1} x \).

The objective of the rescaling is the make the columns of

\[ \begin{bmatrix} \bar P & \bar A^T \\ \bar A & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

have similar \( l_\infty \) norm, and similarly for the columns of

\[ \begin{bmatrix} \bar P & \bar q \end{bmatrix}. \]
