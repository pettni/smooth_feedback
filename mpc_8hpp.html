Convert OptimalControlProblem on \( (\mathbb{G}, \mathbb{U}) \) into a tangent space QuadraticProgram on \( (\mathbb{R}^{\dim \mathfrak{g}}, \mathbb{R}^{\dim \mathfrak{u}}) \).

The OptimalControlProblem is encoded into a QuadraticProgram via linearization around \((g_{lin}(t), u_{lin}(t))\) followed by time discretization. The variables of the QP are

\[ \begin{bmatrix} \mu_0 & \mu_1 & \ldots & \mu_{K - 1} & x_1 & x_2 & \ldots & x_K \end{bmatrix}, \]

where the discrete time index \(k\) corresponds to time \(t_k = k \frac{T}{K} \) for \( k = 0, 1, \ldots, K \).

The resulting QP has \( K \dim \mathfrak{g} + K \dim \mathfrak{u} \) variables and \( 2 K \dim \mathfrak{g} + K \dim \mathfrak{u} \) constraints.

Note
Given a solution \((x^*, \mu^*)\) to the QuadraticProgram, the corresponding solution to the OptimalControlProblem is \( u^*(t) = u_{lin}(t) \oplus \mu^*(t) \) and the optimal trajectory is \( g^*(t) = g_{lin}(t) \oplus x^*(t) \).
Constraints are added as \( g_{min} \ominus g_{lin} \leq x \leq g_{max} \ominus g_{lin} \) and similarly for the input. Beware of using constraints on non-Euclidean spaces.
f and glin must be differentiable with the default smooth::diff method. If using an automatic differentiation method this means that the functions must be templated on the scalar type.
